What is poutine?

Poutine is a dish composed of fries and topped with cheese curds and gravy.

At most poutine is a carby, cheesy, brown gravy-loaded masterpiece. There are no rules when it comes to poutine and can be topped with veggies, bacon, chili, anything!

Ingredients

(For one person)

  • 1 Quart vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 (10.25 ounce) can beef gravy
  • 5 medium potatoes, cut into fries
  • 2 cups cheese curds

Steps to make Poutine

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer or deep skillet to 365 degrees F (185 degrees C)
  2. While the oil is heating, begin to warm gravy.
  3. Place fries into the hot oil, and cook until light brown, 8 to 10 minutes. Cook fries in batches in necessary to allow room to move a little in the oil. Remove to a paper towel plate to drain.
  4. Place fries on a your serving plate and sprinkle cheese over them.
  5. Ladle warmed gravy over the fries and cheese, and serve.
